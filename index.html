How to use this page

One-time setup

  1. Open this page in iPhone Safari, tap Share → Add to Home Screen. Launch from the Home Screen icon to run offline.
  2. Open Settings (⚙) → GitHub Sync. Owner, repo, branch, and path are already filled in — just paste your access token once, tap Test connection → Save. This device remembers it; you won't enter it again unless you clear the browser.

Capturing

  1. Tap the big Start GPS button on first launch. Grant permission. The accuracy badge in the top bar turns green when you're within the gate (default 5 m).
  2. Pick a Group chip and a Confidence. Tap the 📷 chip if you want a photo attached to the next capture.
  3. Pick a mode: ● Point averages 8 fixes for one precise spot. ▱ Area / ／ Line drop one vertex per tap of the big button — tap ✓ when done. ↝ Track records continuously as you walk: tap ● REC, walk the driveway/path, tap ■ STOP at the end (good for curves). ↔ Tape opens the compass for offset-from-anchor.
  4. Use ↶ to undo the last vertex (or the last point if no shape is in progress).

The map

  • Drag with one finger to pan, pinch to zoom.
  • ⤢ fits all captures + your current position. ⊙ centers on your live fix.
  • Points are colored by confidence. The blue pulsing dot is your live GPS position; the dim circle around it is the reported horizontal accuracy.

Sync

  • The ⚡ icon in the top bar shows sync state. Tap it to open settings or force a sync.
  • If offline, captures stay queued and upload when the phone reconnects.
  • Photos are stored as thumbnails inside the GeoJSON (base64 data URLs). For very many photos, consider exporting and clearing periodically.

Gotchas

  • Phone elevation is ignored on purpose. Elevation is attached later from USGS 3DEP/EPQS data per point.
  • Shape vertices aren't averaged. For corners that must be both precise and part of a polygon, capture as Points and reconstruct downstream.
  • Tape offsets inherit the anchor's accuracy. Don't tape from a "rough" anchor and call the result precise.
